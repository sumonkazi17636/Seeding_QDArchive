Understanding and measuring responsiveness of human resources for health in rural Bangladesh




Please cite any data used from this collection as follows: 



Joarder, Taufique. (2017) "Understanding and measuring responsiveness of human resources for health in rural Bangladesh." Data Collection, QDR:10086. Syracuse, NY: Qualitative Data Repository [distributor]. https://doi.org/10.5064/F6P55KNC

Responsiveness of human resources for health (HRH) is defined as the social actions that health providers do to meet the legitimate expectations of service seekers. It may dissuade patients from early care seeking, diminish their interest in adopting preventive health information, decrease trust with health service providers, and marginalize at-risk population groups. The overall goal of this research was to examine HRH responsiveness in rural Bangladesh, to develop a scale to measure the responsiveness, and finally to demonstrate the application of the measurement method.

DATA PROCESSING NOTE

The data were processed according to the Qualitative Data Repository’s curation policy. All files were checked for integrity. Files were checked to make sure they match documentation and consistent file names were applied. Any data or documentation that breached confidentiality rules were altered or suppressed to preserve confidentiality: specific workplaces, places of residency were redacted, and graduation dates were replaced by date ranges. Given the language of the transcripts, all such redactions were performed by the depositor in close consultation with QDR.

Files were converted from .doc and .docx formats to PDF/A using Adobe Acrobat Pro DC Version 2015.006.30279. Bangla transcripts were checked for de-identification in consultation with the depositor as well as using machine translation.
