Presidential Campaign Advertising in Chile, Brazil, and Peru.




Please cite any data used from this collection as follows: 



Boas, Taylor. (2015) “Presidential Campaign Advertising in Chile, Brazil, and Peru.” Data Collection, QDR:10053. Syracuse, NY: Qualitative Data Repository [distributor]. http://doi.org/10.5064/F62Z13FQ

Videos of presidential campaign advertising were collected in order to characterize the evolution of campaign strategies in Latin American new democracies. The author aimed to collect every piece of televised campaign advertising broadcast by a major presidential candidate in Chile, Brazil, and Peru used from the 1980s through 2011 (with the exception of Peru’s 1995 and 2000 elections). Major candidates are defined as the first- and second-place finishers, plus any third candidate who received over 20% of the valid vote.

Only a sub-set of the full data collection of over a thousand videos is shared here. A decision about the online accessibility of the full set of project files is pending, awaiting further investigation of their provenance (originally broadcast publicly on television). Interested registered users can contact QDR to arrange viewing all videos on site.
