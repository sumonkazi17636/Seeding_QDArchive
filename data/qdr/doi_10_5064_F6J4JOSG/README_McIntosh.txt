**Description**
Project  Overview
This project aims to bridge this gap by involving a variety of stakeholders to identify, understand, and create actionable guidance that assists stakeholders in navigating the ethical landscape of academia-industry neurotechnology partnerships.
All participants (from the seven groups: patients, neuroethicists, institutional officials (industry-cultivating and regulatory) clinicians, researchers, industry representatives) completed a screening and demographic survey via Qualtrics and participated in a one-hour interview conducted over Zoom. Prior to the interviews, the patient group received brief educational background on academia-industry partnerships to support informed discussion. Interviews were led by trained interviewers who obtained verbal consent and followed a semi-structured interview guide tailored to each stakeholder group. The interviews covered five main domains:
Prior experiences with academia-industry partnerships,
ethical and practical considerations,
policy and regulatory frameworks,
resources to support responsible partnerships, and advice for future stakeholders.
The project data shared here contain the following de-identified materials:
1.       Survey Data: Responses to pre-interview screening and demographic surveys from CLN, SCI, IND, PAT, NET, CIO, and RIO stakeholders.
2.       Interview Transcripts: De-identified transcripts of one-hour stakeholder interviews.
Supporting documentation includes the interview guides used for each stakeholder group, survey questionnaires / codebooks, and qualitative codebooks used for initial thematic analysis. Also included are interview information slides and the consent forms used for the patients and neuroethicists groups.
