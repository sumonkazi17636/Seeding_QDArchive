Negotiating Formality: Informal Sector, Market, and State in Peru.




Please cite any data used from this collection as follows: 



Roever, Sally. (2015) “Negotiating Formality: Informal Sector, Market, and State in Peru.” Topic Cluster, QDR:10067. Syracuse, NY: Qualitative Data Repository [distributor]. http://doi.org/10.5064/F6D798BB

This project explores the political roots of regulation in the informal economy in Lima, Peru.  Analysis is based on a 1,000-page paper-based archive collected between 2011 and 2012, about the evolution of street commerce in the neighborhood of La Victoria. The archive includes newspaper clippings, magazines, newsletters, fliers, records from street vending organizations, records from attempts to implement key regulations in the mid-1990s, and other materials about the politics of street vending. The sources were organized into three main categories: laws and by-laws; organizations; and background documents. All the shared documents have been digitized by QDR.

The data include internal documents of street vending organizations and public domain materials. Any documents that include proper names of people or organizations must be anonymized to protect the identity of the vendors involved and will be added as a second version of the collection. Standard QDR access for all files currently shared.
